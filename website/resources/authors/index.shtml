Authors

Rosegarden has three core developers, who between them are to blame for most of the application as you find it now. Their email addresses are shown below in obfuscated form - change the "at" to "@" and the "dot" to "." to form the valid addresses.

Chris Cannam, cannam at all-day-breakfast dot com

Chris has been working on one incarnation or another of Rosegarden since the end of 1993. It was then a university project for two students, devised by professor John Fitch at the University of Bath, with Andy Green working on the MIDI sequencer and Chris on the notation editor. They were the sole developers until late in 1995, some time after the barely-usable 1.0 release.

For Rosegarden-4, Chris is responsible for most of the notation rendering and some of the file and data code. He lives in London and is currently working full-time on Rosegarden and related activities.

Richard Bown, bownie at bownie dot com

Richard has been working on Rosegarden since the end of 1995, when he was drafted in to take over the Unix sequencer. He is responsible for almost all the MIDI, audio, sequencing and recording code in Rosegarden-4.

Richard works full-time on Rosegarden and related music and technology projects and is available to talk to about commercial and non-commercial activities involving music technology (including Rosegarden) and also to give demos.

Guillaume Laurent, glaurent at telegraph-road dot org

Guillaume lives in Cannes, in the South of France. He's been using Linux since 1995, and first worked on Rosegarden in the summer of 1996, when he supplied a few hundred lines of new code in his first couple of emails. Guillaume is responsible for getting the Rosegarden-4 project restarted, for creating the basic design and writing much of the GUI code and file and data framework, and for most of the project administration.

He has also helped maintain Gtk-- for a while, and is involved in some parts of KDE here and there. In his spare time, he works as a software engineer.

Friends of Rosegarden

There is an ever-growing list of people who are helping out with coding, documenting, translating and testing who also merit a mention and can be said to be true friends of Rosegarden. For the moment you can keep up to date with who the core contributors are by having a look at the latest AUTHORS file.
