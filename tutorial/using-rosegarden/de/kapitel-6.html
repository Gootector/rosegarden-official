6 You Don't Know Jack

Jack is short for the Jack Audio Connection Kit. This marvel is at the heart of Linux audio, and it allows for a level of interoperability between applications that's undreamt of in the world of proprietary software. Jack is the cement that binds together a host of varied applications, and lets them all work together seamlessly.

This chapter will discuss starting the jackd server, configuring connections with qjackctl, and getting a variety of applications such as Timidty, QSynth, Hydrogen, JamIn, and ZynAddSubFX to play nicely together. I'm under severe time constraints at the moment, and I don't have time to do this very complex subject justice before my deadline.
