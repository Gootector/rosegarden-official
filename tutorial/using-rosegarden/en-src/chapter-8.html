Notation (under heavy construction)

Rosegarden has what might well be the most capable notation editor ever found in a MIDI sequencer. Typically, applications which do the best job of notation are dedicated entirely to that pursuit, and they have very limited, rudimentary MIDI capabilities. On the other side of that coin, applications which do the best job as MIDI sequencers often have extremely poor notation editors. Rosegarden straddles these two extremes very well, and its notation editor packs quite a surprising amount of punch.

One of the most striking features is the fact that Rosegarden maintains both a display duration and a performance duration for every note. Users typically have to make a hard choice between a human performance and legible notation. I myself am certainly no stranger to the practice of maintaining both a quantized and an un-quantized version of a piece in separate tracks or separate files. Rosegarden allows you to have the best of both worlds, maintaining legible notation and human performance within the very same set of events.

Notation Editor Overview

In order to open the notation editor, you must have at least one segment available. You can open any number of segments on any number of tracks simultaneously by selecting the segment or segments you wish to edit, and then right clicking on one of them and choosing "Open in Notation Editor" from the context menu.

Additionally, the notation editor is the normal default, so you can also open it either by pressing Enter, or by double clicking on a segment. (You can configure this behavior from Settings -> Configure Rosegarden.)
