Mailing Lists

Much discussion about using, developing and generally living with Rosegarden-4 goes on in our mailing lists.

For problems relating to the installation, building or using of Rosegarden-4 go to the rosegarden-user mailing list. Check out the rosegarden-users archives too.

If you're a developer and interested in finding out more about the development of our project and perhaps even joining in then there's the rosegarden-devel mailing list.

Authors

Rosegarden-4 has three core developers, who between them are to blame for most of the application as you find it now. Their email addresses are shown below in obfuscated form - change the "at" to "@" and the "dot" to "." to form the valid addresses.

Chris Cannam

Chris is one of the grandfathers of the Rosegarden project. He's a software developer who lives in London.

Chris first started working on Rosegarden at the end of 1993. It was then a university project for two students, devised by professor John Fitch at the University of Bath, with Andy Green working on the MIDI sequencer and Chris on the notation editor. They were the sole developers until late in 1995, some time after the barely-usable 1.0 release. For Rosegarden-4, Chris is responsible for most of the notation rendering and some of the file and data code.

email: cannam at all-day-breakfast dot com

Richard Bown

Richard is a Linux music advocate and software developer who also lives in London. He's been working on Rosegarden since the end of 1995, when he was drafted in to take over the Unix sequencer. He is responsible for almost all the MIDI, audio, sequencing and recording code in Rosegarden-4.

email address: bownie at bownie dot com

Guillaume Laurent

Guillaume lives in Cannes, in the South of France. He's been using Linux since 1995, and first worked on Rosegarden in the summer of 1996, when he supplied a few hundred lines of new code in his first couple of emails. Guillaume is responsible for getting the Rosegarden-4 project restarted, for creating the basic design and writing much of the GUI code and file and data framework, and for most of the project administration.

He has also helped maintain Gtk-- for a while, and is involved in some parts of KDE here and there. In his spare time, he works as a software engineer.

email: glaurent at telegraph-road dot org
