Bug Priorities

Bugs should be submitted with a default priority of 5. A new priority will be assigned once someone has examined the report.

Priority

Meaning

9

critical - crashes, build problems, and data corruption/bad behavior problems which impact core functionality for a large number of users (eg. starting without JACK crashes; someone forgot to commit the new Blurfl class and broke CVS; something broke to such an extent that data was either ruined forever, or had to be salvaged by manually hacking the XML; doing the wrong thing causes 300 error dialogs to pop up); outstanding issues that simply must be dealt with before we can release again

8

grave - repeatable crashes which can be worked around or avoided, and other very significant usability problems (eg. crash when inserting triplets this way, but you can still get them in another way; audio loops or the solo button or something similarly critical is broken badly)

7

serious - less significant usability problems, broken features

6

acknowledged – we'll do something about this sooner

5

unclassified

4

acknowledged – we'll do something about this later

3

someday - this is a real bug, and we really should fix it, but we just don't have the manpower to look at this anytime in the foreseeable future

2

minor - unrepeatable crashes and other random flake that you probably woudn't have reported if you had read the guidelines at http://rosegarden.sourceforge.net/bug-guidelines.html

1

nuisance - trivial complaints that are just barely on the bug side of the

bug/RFE divide, and/or which shouldn't really have been submitted at all, but we're feeling too polite at the moment to delete them outright
